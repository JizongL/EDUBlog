### Definiton:
Let S be a nonempty subset of a group G. The subgroup generated by S, $$\text{<S>}$$ is the smallest subgroup of G that contains the set S, in the sense that if H is a subgroup fo G and H contains S, then H contains $$\text{<S>}$$

Note: Let S be a noneempty subset of a group G. Let $$\{H_i: i \in I\}$$ represent the set of all subgroups of G that contain the set S.

1: Then $$\text{<S>} = \bigcap_{i \in I} H_i$$

2: $$\text{<S>} = \{a_1^{n_1},a_2^{n_2},...,a_k^{n_k} \lvert a_i= S, n_i \in \mathbb{Z} \quad \text{not fixed} \ 1≤i≤k\}$$

### Definition:

Let G be a group. The set $$\mathbb{Z}(G)= \{a \in G \lvert ax=xa, \text{for all} \ x \in G\}$$ is called the center of the group G.

### Theorem 3.5

The center of a group G is a subgroup of G.

**Proof**

Let G be a group with identity e, then $$ex=x$$ and $$xe=x$$, so $$ex=xe$$ for all $$x \in G$$, Hence $$e \in \mathbb{Z}(G)$$

(Closure) Let $$a, b \in \mathbb{Z}(G)$$. then ax=xa and xb=bx for all x in G,

Hence $$(ab)x=a(bx)= a(xb)=(ax)b=(xa)b=x(ab)$$ , for all $$x \in G$$,

Thus $$ab \in \mathbb{Z}(G)$$ and $$\mathbb{Z}(G)$$ is closed under the operation of G.

(show $$a^{-1} \in \mathbb{Z}(G))$$ Since $$a \in \mathbb{Z}(G)$$

$$ax=xa$$, for all $$x \in G$$, so

$$a^{-1}(ax)a^{-1}=a^{-1}(xa)a^{-1}$$

$$(a^{-1}a)(x a^{-1})=(a^{-1}x)(aa^{-1})$$

$$e(xa^{-1})=(a^{-1}x)e$$

$$xa^{-1}=a^{-1}x$$ for all $$x \in G$$

Therefore, $$a^{-1} \in \mathbb{Z}(G)$$

By theorem 3.2, $$\mathbb{Z}(G)$$ is a subgroup of G.

### Definition(Centralizer of an element)

Let a be a fixed element of a group G, the centralizer of a in G, $$C(a)$$ is the set of all elements of G that commute with a.

### Theorem 3.6.
